Epidemiological Model

This model is designed to provide a better understanding of the ways by which infectious agents spread. Additionally, the model provides an insight into how variables such as population density, length of infectious period, and interventions affect spread. The R0 is not a value which is totally intrinsic to the contagion, but is determined by population density, counter measures, and environmental factors, as well as the characteristics of the infectious agent. The 'Spread Rate' controls the speed at which the simulation subjects move relative to elapsed time. Days are simulated in 5 second intervals. The graph of 'New Daily Infections' is displayed when the animation is stopped. The 'Variation in Infectious Period' is intended to approximate the statistical variations from the average infectious period of a given contagion (extrema), and is accomplished by a random function.
